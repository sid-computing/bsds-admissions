01 / Admissions

The admission process,
made understandable.

This website is intended primarily for Class XI and XII students interested in pursuing the Bachelor of Statistical Data Science at the Indian Statistical Institute.

Admission is conducted for the BSDS programme as a whole. Students later receive a centre according to the applicable allocation process, their merit position, category and submitted preferences.

Admission mode

Maximum Mathematics percentile from JEE Main or CUET-UG

The Mathematics percentile used for admission is the higher of the candidate’s eligible JEE Main and CUET-UG Mathematics percentiles, subject to the official rules for the relevant admission cycle.

Always check the latest official admission notice for eligibility, accepted examination years, application dates, document requirements and allocation rules.

Typical admission journey

From application to allocation

This is a simplified overview. Exact procedures and deadlines should always be confirmed from the official notice for that year.

STEP 01

Check eligibility

Confirm the academic, subject and examination requirements published for the current admission cycle.

STEP 02

Apply to ISI

Complete the BSDS application and provide the required examination information and supporting documents.

STEP 03

Receive a merit position

Merit lists are prepared according to the announced admission rules and applicable reservation provisions.

STEP 04

Submit preferences

Centre preferences may affect the centre eventually allocated, subject to merit position, category and seat availability.

STEP 05

Follow allocation notices

Check every allocation notice carefully and complete the required response within the specified deadline.

Delhi Centre intake

A small batch
of 25 students.

The Bachelor of Statistical Data Science at the ISI Delhi Centre has an annual intake of 25 students.

A small batch can shape classroom interaction, peer learning and the overall student experience. The final category-wise allocation remains subject to official reservation and admission rules.

05 / Before joining

Arrive curious,
not overwhelmed.

You do not need to complete an entire university curriculum before joining. A strong foundation and willingness to learn are more valuable than rushing through advanced material.

Mathematics

Revise algebra, functions, coordinate geometry, calculus and basic combinatorial reasoning.

Programming

Familiarity with variables, conditionals, loops and functions in Python or R can make the transition easier.

Statistics

Develop intuition for data, averages, variation, probability and the interpretation of tables and graphs.

Study habits

Be prepared for proof-based mathematics, programming practice, projects and learning some concepts independently.

Moving to Delhi

Confirm accommodation, estimate monthly expenses, understand your route to the centre and prepare the documents required during registration.
