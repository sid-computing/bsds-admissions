01 / Admissions

The 2027 process,
made understandable.

This guide explains the JEE Main route. Admission is conducted for BSDS as a whole; centre allocation depends on the applicable rules, merit position, category, preferences and seat availability.

Scope of this guide

ISI also accepts eligible CUET-UG scores. Because this guide focuses on JEE Main, CUET-UG applicants should consult the latest official ISI admission notice for their scoring and tie-breaking rules.

Official rule · 2027 cycle

Eligibility through JEE Main

Candidates may use an eligible JEE Main 2026 or JEE Main 2027 scorecard.

They must have passed Class XII in 2026 or 2027 with Mathematics, English and three other subjects, and must have obtained an aggregate score of at least 75%.

Merit-list calculation

Q: Mathematics percentile

For the JEE Main route, the primary merit score is the candidate’s eligible JEE Main Mathematics percentile.

Q = JEE Main Mathematics percentile

Candidates with a higher value of Q are placed higher on the merit list.

First tie-breaker: T1

If candidates have the same Q score, their overall JEE Main percentiles are compared.

T1 = JEE Main overall percentile

The candidate with the higher value of T1 is placed higher.

Second tie-breaker: T2

If the tie remains, candidates are compared by age as on 31 May 2027.

T2 = Age as on 31 May 2027

The candidate with the lower value of T2—the younger candidate—is placed higher.

Merit-list order

  1. 1
    Higher Q

    Higher JEE Main Mathematics percentile

  2. 2
    Higher T1

    Higher JEE Main overall percentile

  3. 3
    Lower T2

    Younger candidate as on 31 May 2027

Time-sensitive information

These requirements describe the 2027 admission cycle. Future applicants must check the official notice for their year before relying on them.

05 / Before joining

Arrive curious,
not overwhelmed.

You do not need to finish a university curriculum before joining. Strong foundations and a willingness to learn matter more.

01

Mathematics

Revise algebra, functions, coordinate geometry, calculus and basic combinatorial reasoning.

02

Programming

Familiarity with variables, conditionals, loops and functions in Python or R can ease the transition.

03

Statistics

Build intuition for data, averages, variation, probability, tables and graphs.

04

Study habits

Prepare for proof-based mathematics, programming practice, projects and independent learning.

05

Moving to Delhi

Confirm accommodation, estimate expenses, understand your route and prepare registration documents.
